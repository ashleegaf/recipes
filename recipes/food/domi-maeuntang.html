Domi Maeuntang

Photo by Korean Bapsang

Description

Domi Maeuntang (pronounced doh-mi may-eun-tong) is the Korean name for spicy seafood stew with snapper fish as the star of the show. Domi means snapper, while maeuntang translates to spicy soup or stew. As a big fan of spicy food, fish, and soups, I loved the soup from first taste, and am always excited when I happen to see it on the menu at Korean restaurants. The recipe is too delicious to not share with the world. If you also love fish soup or enjoy eating spicy food, keep reading!

Ingredients

For the Soup

  • Snapper
  • Korean radish
  • Tofu
  • Zucchini
  • Scallion
  • Ssukgat

For the Seasoning Mix

  • Gochujang
  • Doenjang
  • Gochugaru
  • Fish sauce
  • Garlic
  • Ginger

For the Broth

  • Anchovy broth

Steps

  1. Cut radish, tofu, zucchini, and scallion into small pieces and set aside on a plate.
  2. Prepare the seasoning by mixing gochujang, doenjang, gochugaru, fish sauce, garlic, and ginger together in a bowl.
  3. Bring anchovy broth to a boil using medium heat.
  4. While the broth is heating up, cut the snapper into small pieces.
  5. After the anchovy broth is brought to a boil, add the seasoning mix, tofu, and mu. Stir. Cover the pot.
  6. After ~5 minutes, add the zucchini and snapper. Stir. ~3 minutes later, add the scallion.
  7. After ~3 minutes, remove the pot from the boiler and add ssukgat.
  8. Soup is ready to eat!
