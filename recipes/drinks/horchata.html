Horchata

Photo by Honduras Tips

Description

Horchata (pronounced or-CHA-tah) is a rice drink consumed in various Central American countries and brought to the region by European conquistadors, who were introduced to the drink during conquests in Africa. In each of the regions horchata was transported to, it was adapted for use with local seeds. A key feature of horchata for me is the absence of dairy in its most traditional state, which elevates the drink to star status in my mind (and stomach)! While restaurants sometimes add cream and/or milk to produce a more creamy drink, doing so is not required to make the drink delicious. Horchata also stands out for being a rich source of various vitamins and minerals owing to the presence of various nut seeds. And with that said, keep reading for a refreshing, Honduran-style horchata!

Ingredients

  • Water
  • Rice
  • Cinnamon sticks
  • Pumpkin seeds
  • Morro seeds
  • Peanuts (no shell)

Steps

  1. Wash the rice twice and blend it with water until fully grounded.
  2. Toast the cinnamon sticks, pumpkin seeds, morro seeds, and peanuts in a skillet until brown.
  3. Add the toasted ingredients and water to the blender filled with ground rice.
    4. Blend until it becomes thick. Add water if needed.
  4. Horchata is ready to drink!
